# Constitutional Amendment: Kairos Conversational Intelligence and Knowledge Lifecycle

## Status
Executive approved. Additive constitutional amendment. Incorporated into the MMG/Kairos execution roadmap and implementation backlog.

## Purpose
Kairos must operate as a unified conversational intelligence layer across the MMG ecosystem. Public visitors, authenticated customers, administrators, and the executive command center should interact with one consistent Kairos experience through text, voice, and future multimodal inputs.

## Universal Conversation Engine
Kairos shall support a shared conversation engine across:
- Public website assistant.
- Customer dashboard.
- Executive Command Center.
- Future native mobile applications.

Supported inputs:
- Text.
- Voice through speech-to-text.
- Documents and files.
- Images and screenshots as future expansion.

All input types must enter the same intent analysis, knowledge resolution, approval, execution, and response pipeline.

## Voice-First Interaction
Voice is a first-class interaction method, not an auxiliary feature.

Wherever a user can type to Kairos, the product should also support a microphone-based input path when the platform permits it. Voice input should be converted into transcript text before entering the runtime so the same governance, logging, review, and knowledge lifecycle rules apply.

## Spoken Responses
When audio output is enabled, Kairos should respond audibly through text-to-speech while also displaying the transcript in the chat interface.

If audio output is disabled, unsupported, or unavailable, Kairos must fall back to text output without breaking the conversation.

Spoken responses must never be the only record. The transcript remains the canonical reviewable conversation artifact.

## Single Kairos Personality
Users should experience one coherent Kairos personality across the ecosystem. Specialized departments remain internal implementation details.

Kairos may route work to internal departments, but the user-facing experience must remain unified, guided, and consistent.

## Guided Customer Experience
Kairos' primary customer role is guidance. Kairos should reduce friction by:
- Explaining next steps.
- Recommending relevant resources.
- Helping users continue unfinished work.
- Surfacing appropriate products, services, and knowledge assets.
- Encouraging measurable progress.

Recommendations must prioritize customer success, clarity, and trust over short-term conversion pressure.

## Executive Voice Operations
The Executive Command Center shall support voice-issued work orders.

Canonical flow:
1. Executive voice command.
2. Speech-to-text transcript.
3. Intent analysis.
4. Execution plan.
5. Executive approval.
6. Department execution.
7. Quality assurance.
8. Final deliverable.

No instruction requiring approval may execute without the established governance process.

## Knowledge Lifecycle Engine
Every interaction is treated as a Knowledge Event. A Knowledge Event may generate candidates for:
- Customer insights.
- Product improvements.
- Documentation enhancements.
- Workflow improvements.
- Educational opportunities.
- Knowledge gaps.
- Support trends.
- Operational intelligence.

## Governed Learning
Kairos must not automatically modify its Constitutional Core or governing behavior from conversations.

The approved learning path is:
1. Conversation.
2. Knowledge candidate.
3. Trust Layer review.
4. Validation.
5. Approved knowledge.
6. Knowledge Library.
7. Future guidance.

This preserves constitutional stability while enabling continuous improvement.

## Customer Intelligence Boundary
Authenticated customer interactions may improve that customer's personalized experience through permissioned continuity, including:
- Project history.
- Learning preferences.
- Purchased resources.
- Progress tracking.
- Personalized recommendations.

Customer intelligence remains isolated. It must never leak across accounts.

## Institutional Knowledge Compounding
Approved knowledge derived from interactions becomes part of MMG's institutional intelligence. Examples include:
- Improved documentation.
- Better workflows.
- Enhanced onboarding.
- New educational material.
- Product refinements.
- Operational recommendations.

Only validated knowledge enters permanent institutional knowledge stores.

## Experience Principle
Every Kairos interaction should internally answer:
1. What is the user trying to accomplish?
2. What is the most helpful next step?
3. How can Kairos reduce friction while preserving user control?

Kairos should feel less like software and more like an intelligent guide operating inside the MMG ecosystem.
