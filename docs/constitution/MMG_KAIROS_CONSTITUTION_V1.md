# MMG / Kairos Constitution v1.0

**Status:** Canonical constitutional baseline — refrozen  
**Effective date:** 2026-07-10  
**Repository:** `infomindsetmediagroup-cloud/MMG-IOS`  
**Authority:** Executive-approved MMG/Kairos doctrine accumulated through July 10, 2026

## 1. Constitutional purpose

This Constitution defines the enduring purpose, operating identity, architectural boundaries, product doctrine, governance principles, and execution obligations of Mindset Media Group and Kairos.

It supersedes fragmented conversational summaries as the primary consolidated constitutional reference. Existing specialized constitutional documents remain authoritative within their scopes unless they conflict with this Constitution, in which case this Constitution governs and the conflict must be resolved through an explicit amendment.

Future constitutional changes are exceptional events requiring explicit executive approval. Ordinary implementation work must extend this baseline rather than reopening foundational architecture.

## 2. Canonical enterprise hierarchy

- **The Legacy, LLC** is the legal parent company.
- **Mindset Media Group (MMG)** is the ecosystem, publishing, education, products, services, and platform umbrella.
- **Kairos** is the intelligence operating system and executive orchestration layer for the MMG ecosystem.
- **ME — Mindset Excellence** is the future community, collaboration, discovery, and networking layer.
- **Recovery Intelligence Platform** is the first major post-Kairos application initiative and reuses the Kairos platform foundation.

MMG is the ecosystem. Kairos is not the ecosystem; Kairos coordinates and strengthens it.

## 3. Foundational purpose and brand doctrine

MMG exists to help people discover, organize, develop, preserve, and share the value already present in their knowledge, experience, skills, creativity, and perspective.

Approved foundational statements:

- **Your Knowledge Has Value.**
- **Helping you discover it, build it, and share it with the world.**
- **We’re not gatekeepers. We’re door openers.**
- **Knowledge grows when it’s shared. Opportunity grows when doors are opened.**

MMG practices knowledge stewardship rather than knowledge gatekeeping. The company must reduce friction, expand opportunity, build trust, and help people become more capable rather than more dependent.

## 4. Experience-first doctrine

The Command Center, Customer Portal, Design Studio, Knowledge Library, website, applications, and future MMG experiences must function as one coherent ecosystem rather than a collection of disconnected tools.

Users should primarily express objectives. Kairos should translate objectives into governed execution by consulting authoritative MMG knowledge, doctrines, specifications, user context, project state, permissions, and available capabilities.

The platform must continually remove unnecessary clicks, repeated input, prompt memorization, fragmented navigation, duplicate decisions, and workflow interruptions.

As capability grows, the customer experience should become simpler.

## 5. Progress and momentum doctrine

Visible progress is foundational.

The platform must show meaningful forward movement through:

- completed assets,
- milestones,
- project advancement,
- knowledge growth,
- published work,
- business accomplishments,
- customer outcomes,
- and durable value creation.

Dashboards must prioritize objectives, progress, blockers, approvals, recommended next actions, and recent wins over feature navigation.

## 6. Kairos canonical role

Kairos is a governed executive coordination layer. Kairos must remain relatively thin.

Kairos is responsible for:

- interpreting objectives,
- assembling context,
- planning,
- routing work,
- coordinating departments and platform services,
- applying governance,
- verifying results,
- explaining outcomes,
- preserving continuity,
- and recommending the next action.

Kairos must not become the sole owner of all business logic. Business rules belong in platform services and domain departments. AI models are replaceable reasoning components, not the constitutional center of the platform.

## 7. Canonical execution lifecycle

Every meaningful objective follows the governed lifecycle:

1. **Observe** — gather facts and current state.
2. **Understand** — identify the true objective, context, constraints, and success criteria.
3. **Decide** — select the governed plan and required capabilities.
4. **Execute** — perform work through approved services and departments.
5. **Verify** — confirm completeness, quality, security, and evidence.
6. **Preserve** — store valuable outcomes, decisions, knowledge, assets, and audit history.
7. **Improve** — refine workflows, estimates, templates, and platform capability.

Conversation is not the system of record. Durable work must become persistent objects.

## 8. Objective-driven operating model

The fundamental unit of value is a **meaningful objective completed with durable value**.

Canonical hierarchy:

`Strategic Initiative → Objective → Project → Milestone → Task → Asset / Deliverable → Evidence → Outcome`

Every task should trace upward to an objective. Every strategic initiative should trace downward to active work.

The principal platform KPI is meaningful objectives completed per unit of human effort, qualified by quality, trust, and durable value.

## 11. Departments and capability contracts

Departments are specialist business modules coordinated by Kairos.

Each department must declare:

- purpose,
- capabilities,
- accepted inputs,
- produced outputs,
- permissions,
- policies,
- dependencies,
- emitted and consumed events,
- approvals,
- confidence,
- metrics,
- and ownership.

Initial activation order:

1. Executive Office
2. Project Management / Planning
3. Knowledge Library
4. Publishing
5. Design Studio
6. Marketing
7. Analytics
8. Customer Success

Publishing is the preferred first reference domain for exercising the full platform-services model.

## 12. Trust, security, and progressive autonomy

Security is a Priority 1 constitutional subsystem. Defense in depth, tenant isolation, least privilege, secure sessions, protected secrets, rate limiting, auditability, rollback, recovery, and release gates are mandatory.

Autonomy progresses by earned confidence:

1. Suggest
2. Prepare
3. Execute after approval
4. Execute automatically within explicit policy
5. Continuously optimize within governance boundaries

High-impact actions require approval unless an explicit policy authorizes autonomous execution. Examples include publishing, external communications, spending, billing changes, destructive actions, and permission changes.

## 13. Knowledge and institutional memory

Kairos and MMG preserve four memory layers:

- **Working memory:** current task and active project context.
- **Operational memory:** active workflows, state, blockers, and execution history.
- **Organizational memory:** policies, standards, templates, decisions, and processes.
- **Strategic memory:** purpose, Constitution, roadmap, brand philosophy, and long-term direction.

Every significant decision should preserve context, alternatives, rationale, approver, consequences, and review conditions.

Institutional knowledge must outlive individual people, interfaces, vendors, frameworks, and AI models.

## 14. Design Studio production-only asset doctrine

The Customer Portal Design Studio is a production workspace, not a free standalone creative platform.

Intermediate assets, editable files, layered files, AI generations, templates, drafts, project files, reusable components, source files, and production materials remain inside the MMG/Kairos ecosystem unless explicitly released as approved final deliverables.

The Design Studio must support modular document, image, video, audio, branding, publishing, marketing, conversion, resizing, organization, and production workflows without attempting to replace every professional desktop tool.

## 15. Customer experience and portal doctrine

The Customer Portal is a guided workspace centered on:

- what the customer is building,
- what is progressing,
- what is blocked,
- what requires approval,
- what is complete,
- and what Kairos recommends next.

Tools are contextual. Objectives and momentum are primary.

## 16. Product and application roadmap doctrine

Execution sequence:

1. Bring Kairos into reliable operational status.
2. Complete the MMG website, dashboard, identity, runtime, orchestration, and core platform services.
3. Validate the reference domain and autonomous department workflows.
4. Build the Recovery Intelligence Platform as the first major application after Kairos becomes reliable.
5. Continue toward ME and additional ecosystem applications using the same shared foundation.

## 17. Engineering doctrine

MMG engineering builds enduring organizational capability, not isolated features.

Engineering invariants:

- One source of truth per canonical entity.
- Explicit ownership and boundaries.
- Commands request actions; events report facts.
- Durable work cannot live only in chat.
- Kairos orchestrates; services implement; departments specialize.
- Business logic does not belong in presentation code.
- Dependencies point downward.
- Reuse is preferred over duplication.
- Production evidence drives refinement.
- Every implementation should improve capability, clarity, reliability, reuse, trust, or simplicity.

The repository is the living implementation of the architecture.

## 18. AI engineering pipeline

The canonical development workflow is:

- **ChatGPT** serves as the executive command interface for planning, architecture review, prioritization, specification, work-order generation, and technical stewardship.
- **An AI coding agent such as Codex** serves as the repository execution engine for implementation, tests, refactoring, and prepared changes.
- **GitHub** is the canonical source of truth for code, contracts, history, decisions, pull requests, and release checkpoints.
- **Production services** run through secure server-side APIs. Consumer ChatGPT is not the production runtime.

No AI system may claim external actions were completed without repository, tool, deployment, or other verifiable evidence.

## 19. GitHub and deployment doctrine

During active development:

- batch meaningful changes,
- use non-production branches,
- use `[skip ci]` where appropriate,
- avoid repeated deploy-triggering pushes,
- preserve Actions minutes for deliberate validation,
- use manual release gates where practical,
- keep `main` deployable and recoverable,
- and never expose secrets.

A checkpoint is frozen only after scope, validation, security boundary, rollback path, and remaining limitations are documented.

## 20. Constitutional change control

This Constitution is refrozen as **Constitution v1.0**.

Future amendments require:

1. explicit executive approval,
2. a named amendment scope,
3. documented rationale,
4. repository implementation,
5. impact analysis,
6. validation,
7. and a formal refreeze record.

Ordinary product ideas, UI changes, model changes, integrations, and workflow refinements do not automatically justify constitutional amendments.

## 21. Enduring north star

Build systems that leave people, knowledge, customers, and the organization more capable after every completed objective.

MMG/Kairos must continuously reduce the distance between intention and successful execution while preserving knowledge, strengthening trust, increasing reuse, and opening doors for future generations.
