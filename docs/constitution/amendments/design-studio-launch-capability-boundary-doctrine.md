# MMG/Kairos Constitutional Amendment

## Customer Portal Design Studio Launch Capability Boundary Doctrine

**Status:** Approved constitutional amendment incorporated into Constitution v1.0 and immediately refrozen.

## Purpose

Establish the production capability boundary for the initial MMG/Kairos Customer Portal Design Studio launch and prevent the launch from depending on unavailable GPU-backed generative media infrastructure.

## Initial Launch Scope

The initial Customer Portal Design Studio launch is a governed manuscript-production workspace.

At launch, the Design Studio is authorized to support:

- manuscript upload and intake
- manuscript correction and revision
- spelling, grammar, punctuation, and consistency fixes
- formatting cleanup
- structural editing support
- chapter and section organization
- front matter and back matter preparation
- metadata preparation
- publishing-readiness review
- proofing workflows
- version management
- approval workflows
- project and asset organization
- Kairos-guided production assistance
- preparation of approved final manuscript deliverables

The launch may also support other non-generative text, document, organizational, workflow, and production capabilities that do not require GPU-backed image, video, or audio generation.

## Explicit Launch Exclusions

The initial launch does not include:

- AI image generation
- AI cover generation
- AI illustration generation
- AI video generation
- AI animation generation
- AI voice generation
- AI music generation
- generative inpainting
- generative background replacement
- generative visual expansion
- advanced GPU-dependent media transformation

These capabilities must not be represented as available, implied in customer-facing copy, exposed through inactive controls, or included in launch commitments until an approved media-generation provider is securely integrated, tested, governed, and released.

## Customer-Supplied Cover Artwork Requirement

For initial launch publishing workflows, the customer is responsible for supplying the cover artwork required for the project.

The platform must:

- clearly request customer-provided cover artwork during intake
- identify accepted file types, dimensions, resolution, and other production requirements
- preserve the uploaded artwork inside the customer’s governed MMG/Kairos workspace
- prevent false claims that Kairos generated the artwork
- allow MMG production review, placement, sizing, validation, and approved non-generative preparation of the supplied artwork
- identify missing or unusable cover artwork as a production dependency

The initial launch must not promise original cover creation through AI generation.

## Permitted Cover-Related Operations

Where supported by ordinary application code or approved non-generative services, the Design Studio may perform bounded production operations on customer-supplied cover artwork, including:

- upload and storage
- file validation
- dimension and resolution checks
- placement into approved cover layouts
- scaling and cropping
- format conversion
- print-readiness checks
- proof preparation
- metadata association
- versioning and approval tracking

These operations must not be described as generative cover design when they are based on customer-supplied artwork.

## Modular Provider Doctrine

The Design Studio must remain architecturally capable of adding approved image, video, audio, and media-processing providers later without redesigning the entire Customer Portal.

Kairos remains the orchestration layer and must route each request only to an available, approved capability.

If a requested capability is unavailable, Kairos must:

1. state the limitation clearly
2. avoid fabricating completion
3. identify an approved alternative workflow
4. request any required customer-supplied asset
5. preserve the blocked dependency in the project record

## Customer Experience Requirement

The launch experience must be honest, simple, and production-focused.

Customers should see the capabilities that are actually available. The interface must not create the impression that image or video generation is active when it is not.

The primary launch value proposition is:

- manuscript improvement
- publishing preparation
- organized production progress
- guided review and approval
- dependable handling of customer-supplied assets

## Roadmap Boundary

Generative image, video, audio, and advanced media capabilities remain approved future modules, not launch requirements.

They may be activated only after:

- an approved provider is selected
- secure server-side integration is completed
- usage controls are established
- cost and billing controls are established
- content-safety and rights controls are established
- customer disclosures are established
- output storage and provenance rules are established
- testing and executive approval are completed

## Implementation Directive

All current launch planning, interface design, product copy, intake forms, workflows, feature flags, and customer expectations must conform to this capability boundary.

Any inactive generative-media controls must be removed, hidden, or clearly marked as unavailable and not sold as part of the launch product.

## Constitutional Refreeze

This amendment is incorporated into the MMG/Kairos Constitution v1.0.

The Constitution is immediately refrozen. Future expansion beyond this boundary requires deliberate implementation and explicit executive approval, but does not require redesigning the manuscript-production foundation established here.
