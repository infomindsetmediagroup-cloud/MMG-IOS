# Strategic Planning / Future Blueprints

## Purpose

This file establishes the permanent Strategic Planning / Future Blueprint section for MMG/Kairos. It is the canonical planning asset for approved future initiatives so Kairos can inspect the long-range docket, understand what has already been approved conceptually, and convert the correct initiatives into implementation work orders when prerequisites are complete.

Blueprints are strategic planning assets, not active production tickets by default. A blueprint becomes implementation work only when it is promoted into the active backlog through executive approval and dependency review.

## Canonical Platform Hierarchy

1. The Legacy, LLC — legal parent.
2. Mindset Media Group (MMG) — ecosystem, publishing, education, products, services, and platform umbrella.
3. Kairos — intelligence operating system and orchestration layer.
4. ME (Mindset Excellence) — community, collaboration, discovery, and networking layer.

# Future Project Pipeline

## Purpose

The Future Project Pipeline defines how MMG/Kairos converts approved ideas into organized, executable work without losing strategic context.

## Pipeline Stages

### 1. Concept Capture

A new idea is captured with its name, intent, ecosystem role, strategic value, and relationship to existing MMG/Kairos systems.

### 2. Blueprint Draft

The idea becomes a structured blueprint that records mission, users, capabilities, dependencies, risks, and phase placement.

### 3. Strategic Review

Kairos evaluates whether the blueprint aligns with the MMG Constitution, ecosystem architecture, customer philosophy, and execution sequence.

### 4. Dependency Mapping

Kairos identifies prerequisite systems such as identity, dashboards, knowledge vaults, marketplace components, billing, trust layer, analytics, or external integrations.

### 5. Roadmap Placement

The initiative receives a phase, status, and priority level. Future projects remain non-active until promoted.

### 6. Work Order Conversion

When approved for execution, Kairos converts the blueprint into repository work orders, issues, implementation tasks, acceptance criteria, and validation steps.

### 7. Implementation Governance

Every implementation task must trace back to an approved doctrine, blueprint, roadmap item, or executive decision.

## Status Labels

- `concept` — captured but not structured.
- `blueprinted` — documented and preserved.
- `dependency-review` — awaiting prerequisite review.
- `ready-for-planning` — eligible for implementation planning.
- `active` — promoted into current execution.
- `deferred` — preserved but intentionally delayed.

## Rule

The pipeline protects focus. Approved future projects are preserved, but they do not displace current production priorities unless explicitly promoted.

# Kairos Strategic Planning Office

## Charter

The Strategic Planning Office is the Kairos function responsible for preserving, organizing, and sequencing MMG's future project docket.

## Mission

Maintain a canonical planning repository that allows Kairos to understand approved future initiatives, evaluate readiness, and convert the correct projects into executable work orders at the correct time.

## Responsibilities

- Maintain the master roadmap and future blueprint library.
- Preserve approved initiatives without allowing them to disrupt active execution priorities.
- Track dependencies across MMG, Kairos, ME, the Knowledge Library, marketplace, dashboards, and future applications.
- Recommend when an initiative is ready to move from future blueprint to implementation planning.
- Convert approved blueprints into structured work orders only after dependency and executive approval review.
- Ensure future work remains aligned with the MMG Constitution, execution doctrine, customer philosophy, and platform architecture.

## Governance Rules

- The Strategic Planning Office does not override the Constitution.
- It does not silently promote future initiatives into active development.
- It must distinguish strategic concepts from implementation tickets.
- It must preserve context so approved ideas do not disappear into old conversations.

## Output Types

- Roadmap updates.
- Dependency maps.
- Blueprint summaries.
- Promotion recommendations.
- Work-order drafts.
- Risk and readiness assessments.

## Success Standard

Kairos should always be able to answer: what is on the docket, why it matters, what it depends on, where it sits in the roadmap, and what must happen before execution begins.

# ME (Mindset Excellence) Blueprint

## Status

Future platform initiative. Blueprinted for long-term roadmap preservation. Not an active replacement for current Kairos/MMG production priorities.

## Platform Role

ME is the community, collaboration, discovery, and networking platform within the Mindset Media Group ecosystem.

## Mission

Create a unified community experience where creators, entrepreneurs, learners, professionals, and future MMG users can share knowledge, collaborate, discover resources, and move through the broader MMG ecosystem with Kairos-guided support.

## Strategic Position

ME should not simply imitate a general social network. It should operate as an AI-assisted knowledge and progress network. Traditional social platforms optimize for engagement; ME should optimize for learning, execution, collaboration, discovery, and measurable progress.

## Core Capabilities

- User profiles.
- Creator and business pages.
- Groups and communities.
- Project sharing.
- Knowledge posts and discussions.
- AI-assisted publishing and post refinement.
- Community Q&A.
- Marketplace and service discovery.
- App and tool discovery.
- Kairos-personalized recommendations.
- Progress tracking and milestone sharing.
- Collaboration matching.

## Ecosystem Integration

ME should integrate with:

- MMG website.
- Kairos.
- Knowledge Library.
- Customer dashboard.
- Digital products and publishing workflows.
- Marketplace and services.
- Future MMG applications.
- Recovery Intelligence Platform when that future phase becomes active.

## AI-Curated Discovery

ME should include an active curation layer that recommends useful tools, apps, resources, workflows, and knowledge assets.

Recommendation sources may include:

- MMG applications.
- Trusted third-party applications.
- Popular App Store and Google Play tools.
- Creator tools.
- AI tools.
- Business and productivity software.
- Educational resources.
- Templates.
- Workflows.
- Community picks.
- Editorial MMG selections.
- Kairos-personalized recommendations.

## Curation Standard

Recommendations should not be based only on popularity. ME should prioritize trusted usefulness, quality, fit, privacy/security considerations, cost-value ratio, community signal, editorial judgment, and user goals.

## Relationship to Kairos

Kairos powers intelligence, personalization, summaries, recommendations, learning paths, workflow generation, and discovery logic. ME provides the social and collaborative environment where people interact, share, and build together.

## Relationship to MMG

MMG remains the master brand and platform umbrella. ME is a major application layer inside MMG, not the parent of MMG.

## Long-Term Vision

ME becomes the connective tissue of the MMG ecosystem. Users should be able to discover apps, find collaborators, access knowledge, join communities, publish ideas, receive Kairos guidance, and move between MMG products through one coherent environment.

## Execution Rule

ME remains a future roadmap project until Kairos operational status, the MMG platform foundation, customer identity, Knowledge Library, dashboards, and relevant marketplace/discovery systems are stable enough to support it.
